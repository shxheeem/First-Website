Challenges

I have been working on developing a Supervisory control and data acquisition (SCADA) system involving autonomous drone solutions to provide wind farm operators with constant eyes in the sky with high frequency autonomous inspections along a predetermined flight path around the wind farm. This ensures a steady availability of the digitalised wind turbine data and limits the health and safety risk of carrying out manual rope inspections on windmill turbines.

Right now, installing SCADA systems such as these capable of acquiring constant and reliable data about the structural health of wind turbines can be strategically used to train algorithms capable of predicting when turbine maintenance needs to be scheduled. This in turn would greatly reduce the cost of unscheduled maintenance on wind turbines and decrease long-term failures.

Proposed Technical Implementation -

    Drone system capable of flying around predetermined flight paths with a payload capable of taking high resolution pictures of turbines. - DJI M30 and its docking system.
    Convolution Neural Network (CNN) based image detection system packaged in the cloud capable of receiving and processing the images in real time. -
    Cloud based platform that enables grid operators to view the raw data collected and provides them with powerful analytical tools to make meaningful conclusions with the data presented.
